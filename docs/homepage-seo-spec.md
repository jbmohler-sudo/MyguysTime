# Homepage SEO + Content Specification — My Guys Time

## Objective

Build a homepage that:

* ranks for high-intent contractor search terms
* clearly explains the product in under 30 seconds
* converts visitors into users

This is NOT a generic SaaS homepage.

This is:

* contractor-focused
* crew-based
* payroll-prep positioning

---

## 1. Primary SEO Keywords

These MUST be naturally integrated into headings and body copy:

Primary:

* contractor hour tracking app
* small crew timecard app
* subcontractor 1099 tracker
* roofer payroll calculator

Secondary:

* simple construction payroll prep
* export timesheets to CSV for accountant
* verified labor hour logs

---

## 2. Page Structure + SEO Mapping

### HERO SECTION

#### H1 (must include primary keyword intent)

Track Crew Hours & Prep Payroll in Seconds—Built for Contractors

#### Supporting text

My Guys Time is a simple contractor hour tracking app built for small crews.
Track your guys’ hours, review the week, and export clean totals for your accountant.

#### CTA

Start your week

---

### SUBHEADER (H2)

The bridge between the job site and your accountant

This must reinforce:

* not full payroll
* not complex software
* just the missing middle

---

## 3. HOW IT WORKS (CORE SEO SECTION)

Each step should include keyword variations naturally.

### Step 1 — Enter hours (Truck)

Fast, simple hour tracking for contractors in the field.
Built for real job sites—not office desks.

### Step 2 — Review the week (Office)

See your entire crew in one place.
Perfect for small crew timecard management.

### Step 3 — Adjust & finalize

Handle reimbursements, deductions, and mixed crews.
Works as a simple subcontractor 1099 tracker.

### Step 4 — Export checks

Export timesheets to CSV for your accountant.
No payroll system required.

## 10. COPY RULES

Tone must:

* sound like real contractors
* be direct and practical
* avoid SaaS buzzwords

Use:

* "my guys"
* "review the week"
* "run payroll"
* "check hours"

Avoid:

* enterprise language
* productivity jargon
* overly technical explanations

---

## 11. DESIGN + SEO CONNECTION

Design decisions must support SEO:

* fast load
* clean structure
* clear headings
* no clutter

Avoid:

* heavy animations
* oversized JS bundles
* unnecessary libraries

---

## 12. SUCCESS METRICS

Homepage succeeds if:

* ranks for contractor-related search terms
* user understands product in under 30 seconds
* bounce rate stays low
* users click CTA within first screen

---

## 13. IMPLEMENTATION INSTRUCTIONS FOR CODEX

When building homepage:

* follow structure exactly
* do not add extra sections
* do not create feature overload
* prioritize clarity over completeness
* ensure keywords appear naturally, not stuffed
* maintain fast load performance

This is a focused, conversion-driven homepage—not a full marketing site.
